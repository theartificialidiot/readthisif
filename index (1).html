An Experiential Syllabus
"Everything in life reminds me of a book I once read. Shouldn't it be the other way around?"

"How do I read more? How do I start reading? How do you find so much time to read?"

I get asked questions like this very often. Generally, I tell them about my '3 parallel books' strategy, or talk about my love for the written word, or perhaps even point to the needs of my career as a researcher and educator. However, none of these answers are true — though they are all factually accurate.

Reading is a pleasurable activity, definitely an illuminating one, and often even a desirable one. But reading is rarely done for its own sake.

We read to fulfill a need. Here is how books have helped me cope with life.

Why You Need Reasons To Read

A habit is easier to keep when it has a purpose.

We can easily pick up habits that are instantly rewarding, fun, and socially hip. Reading is rarely instant. So if you're keen on making reading a habit — snag books that speak to your needs, are well loved, and are an absolute joy to dive into.
